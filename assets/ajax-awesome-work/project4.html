SeaOurWorld
by Kaely Mills
At UCF, I graduated with a major in Advertising-Public Relations (Ad-PR). In the program’s capstone course, Campaigns, teams devised fictional campaigns for clients in branding, public relations and advertising efforts. For the final Campaigns project, my team was assigned SeaWorld as a client, a company that’s struggled with public image since the release of the Blackfish documentary in 2016.

Our campaign focused on restoring SeaWorld’s broken image. Our research indicated that although the company had spent millions toward animal research and rescue efforts, it had failed to effectively communicate said efforts. As the copywriter, I fixated my concepts on the compelling stories that emerged from SeaWorld’s rescue efforts: beached whales nursed back to health, penguins trapped in oil spills given a new home, and nearly an entire turtle species saved.
